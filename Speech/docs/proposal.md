Abstract/Tóm tắt 
Large-scale Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) systems, such as Whisper, are being widely deployed but exhibit serious vulnerabilities to adversarial attacks (AA). Most existing attack methods focus solely on degrading accuracy and rely on first-order gradient information, which is often countered by 'gradient obfuscation'. While second-order optimization methods-leveraging the Hessian matrix-offer superior robustness and the ability to penetrate strong defenses, the computational cost of calculating the Hessian for the entire audio input remains a significant hurdle. This project proposes a breakthrough solution: employing a Truncated Hessian to optimize compact Adversarial Patches directly in the time domain. This approach not only reduces computational overhead but also incorporates a multi-objective attack strategy to simultaneously degrade recognition accuracy and exhaust system performance via decoding loop mechanisms. Furthermore, the project introduces a defense mechanism based on regularizing the network’s local curvature within the targeted regions to enhance certified robustness
Key words:  Adversarial Patch, Truncated Hessian, Multi-Objective Attack, Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR), Second-Order Optimization.
1. Introduction / Giới thiệu  
1.1. Literature review / Tình hình nghiên cứu trong và ngoài nước 
Research on Adversarial Attacks (AA) in the audio domain has seen significant advancement. First-order techniques like the Basic Iterative Method (BIM), when applied directly to raw waveforms (1D), have demonstrated superior "stealthiness" (higher SNR) and effectiveness compared to attacks on the frequency spectrum (2D).
Recently, the MORE method has expanded the attack landscape for ASR models (such as Whisper) from single-objective (accuracy degradation) to multi-objective (targeting both accuracy and efficiency). By employing the Repetitive Encouragement Doubling Objective (REDO), MORE forces the model to generate infinitely repeating transcriptions, drastically increasing computational costs.
From an optimization perspective, research on SCORPIO and Curvature-based Robust Training (CRT) has proven that leveraging second-order information (curvature/Hessian) via quadratic approximations or by bounding Hessian eigenvalues helps models circumvent gradient obfuscation, achieve faster convergence, and provide formal robustness certificates
1.2. The necessity of the research / Sự cần thiết tiến hành nghiên cứu
While the MORE multi-objective attack is highly effective, it necessitates applying perturbations across the entire duration of the audio signal. In contrast, localized Adversarial Patches are more practical for real-world deployment but remain difficult to optimize using first-order gradients due to the complex loss landscape. Applying global Hessian (N×N) computation to long audio sequences is computationally infeasible in terms of both memory and time. Therefore, researching a Truncated Hessian mechanism—which approximates the curvature only within the subspace containing the Adversarial Patch—is an urgent requirement. This approach balances the power of second-order optimization with computational efficiency, while opening new avenues for creating Adversarial Patches capable of triggering multi-objective errors (such as the REDO repetition loop) using only a very short segment of noise
2. Research objectives / Mục tiêu của đề tài 
Develop the mathematical foundations and algorithmic framework for the Truncated Hessian method to optimize Adversarial Patches specifically for 1D raw audio waveforms.
Develop a Multi-Objective Adversarial Patch attack (Hessian-Patch-Attack) that integrates local second-order information with the Repetitive Encouragement Doubling Objective (REDO) to simultaneously maximize the Word Error Rate (WER) and exhaust the inference efficiency (latency) of ASR systems.
Propose and verify a novel defense mechanism by regularizing the local curvature restricted to the patch region, aimed at providing efficient certified robustness certificates for large-scale ASR models 
3. Research scope / Phạm vi nghiên cứu 
Target Models: Large-scale Transformer-based ASR architectures, specifically the OpenAI Whisper family (from Tiny to Large variants).
Attack Conditions: White-box threat model in the time domain (1D waveform). The Adversarial Patch size is constrained to be under 10% of the total input signal duration 
4. Approach and Method / Cách tiếp cận và phương pháp nghiên cứu 
A. Second-Order Adversarial Patch Optimization Algorithm. Building on the foundation of the SCORPIO framework, this algorithm optimizes adversarial noise through a quadratic approximate risk function rather than relying solely on first-order gradients like traditional methods.
Addressing Computational Complexity: To avoid the prohibitive cost of computing a full second-order derivative matrix, the project utilizes the Hessian-Vector Product technique via finite difference approximations (specifically the Forward Euler method).
Technical Breakthrough: The direction vector is specifically configured to have non-zero values only within the targeted patch region. This transforms the process into an extremely lightweight operation, minimizing local computational load while retaining the potency of second-order curvature information.
Projection-free Optimization: The algorithm employs the Frank-Wolfe iteration method. This removes the computational overhead of projecting onto complex constraint spaces and eliminates the need for time-consuming step-size tuning during the update process.
B. Multi-Objective Integration. The optimized patch algorithm is integrated into the MORE (Multi-Objective Repetitive Doubling Encouragement) attack framework. The training process for the adversarial patch is divided into two hierarchical stages:
Repulsion Stage (Accuracy Attack): The patch is optimized to maximize classification loss. The goal is to push the model's prediction probability away from the correct labels, destabilizing the initial decoding trajectory and increasing the Word Error Rate.
Anchoring Stage (Efficiency Attack): This stage exploits the autoregressive nature of models like Whisper to exhaust computational resources through two mechanisms:
End-of-Sentence (EOS) Suppression: Penalizing the probability of the token that terminates decoding to force the model to continue generating output indefinitely.
Repetitive Encouragement Doubling Objective (REDO): Periodically creating a new target sequence by doubling the recently decoded text segment. This forces the patch to learn to trigger a self-reinforcing repetition loop. An asymmetric interleaving mechanism is used to update the target cyclically, preventing training instability when processing excessively long sequences.
C. Local Second-Order Defense .Alongside the attack framework, the project proposes a novel defense mechanism by extending the theory of Curvature-based Robust Training (CRT).
Mechanism: While global curvature limits for deep networks are often loose and computationally expensive, this project extracts local curvature bounds exclusively within the subspace of the adversarial patch.
Model Retraining: This localized curvature information is incorporated into the total loss function as a regularization component during model retraining.
Effectiveness: This process forces the model to learn to smooth its decision boundary specifically at positions where  patches are most likely to be inserted. The result is the generation of Robustness Certificates that mathematically prove the recognition system is immune to adversarial patches of a defined maximum size.


5. Expected results / Dự kiến kết quả đề tài 
1. Theoretical Foundation: Mathematical Proof for Loss Surface Optimization using Truncated Hessian for 1D Time-Series Adversarial Patches
The primary objective of the project is to establish a rigorous mathematical foundation to address the prohibitive computational costs of utilizing second-order information. For 1D time-series audio data with extremely high sample counts, computing the complete Hessian matrix (global curvature) is computationally and memory-wise infeasible.
The project provides a mathematical proof for the validity of the Truncated Hessian. By performing a second-order Taylor expansion of the adversarial risk function, we define the adversarial noise as being restricted to a specific localized region through a masking mechanism. The proof demonstrates that the second-order component of the risk can be precisely simplified into a localized operator, which we define as the Truncated Hessian.
Combined with the Hessian-Vector Product (HVP) technique via finite difference approximations (such as the Forward Euler method), the directional vector is configured to have non-zero values only within the targeted patch region. This proves that utilizing local curvature information to optimize the patch is mathematically accurate, enabling rapid convergence on complex loss surfaces without the need for global Hessian computation. Furthermore, attacking directly in the 1D time domain avoids signal distortions and artifacts typically introduced during the inversion process of frequency-based representations.
2. Attack Framework: Multi-Objective Adversarial Patch Framework Surpassing First-Order Gradients
The project develops a breakthrough localized Adversarial Patch framework that directly targets large-scale Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) models such as Whisper. Unlike traditional attack methods that focus solely on degrading accuracy, this framework integrates a multi-objective strategy through the Repetitive Encouragement Doubling Objective (REDO) and End-of-Sentence (EOS) Suppression.
Specifically, the Adversarial Patch is optimized to perform two hierarchical tasks:
Repulsion: Pushing the decoding trajectory away from correct labels to maximize the Word Error Rate (WER) using classification loss.
Anchoring: Triggering self-reinforcing loops by periodically doubling the target sequence and penalizing the probability of the termination token.
The result is that the Whisper system is deceived into generating incorrect, highly repetitive transcriptions with significantly increased lengths, thereby exhausting the system’s computational resources and increasing processing latency.
To optimize this patch, the framework utilizes the Frank-Wolfe algorithm on the previously proven second-order approximation. This algorithm is projection-free and eliminates the need for complex step-size tuning. By leveraging localized curvature information (Truncated Hessian), the framework successfully circumvents "gradient obfuscation," a phenomenon where models create highly non-linear loss landscapes to defeat first-order methods like PGD or FGSM. This ensures superior attack potency compared to traditional gradient-based methods while maintaining extreme computational efficiency by optimizing only within a restricted subspace of the audio.
3. Defense Mechanism: Fine-tuning Whisper with Local Curvature Defense and Curvature-based Robustness Certificates (CRC)
To counter the threat of Adversarial Patches, the project proposes an active defense mechanism integrated directly into the fine-tuning process of the Whisper model. This mechanism is built upon the theory of Curvature-based Robust Training (CRT).
Instead of attempting to limit the curvature bounds of the entire massive network—a task that would result in excessively loose bounds and a decrease in standard accuracy—the project develops a regularizer based on Local Curvature. During training, the upper bounds of the Hessian matrix eigenvalues (representing the curvature) are calculated and penalized exclusively within the subspace corresponding to the regions where patches are most likely to be inserted.
Incorporating this curvature penalty into the total loss function forces Whisper’s decision boundary to become flatter and smoother in these vulnerable regions. The final outcome is that the system provides Curvature-based Robustness Certificates (CRC). Unlike empirical defenses that are easily circumvented by new and stronger attacks, CRC provides a formal mathematical guarantee. It calculates a precise local safety radius within which no adversarial patch is capable of altering the classification output or triggering Whisper’s Repetitive Encouragement Doubling Objective (REDO) loop.
